Follow the money.

How does Parksville's fiscal health compare to similar BC communities? Every number on this page comes from official provincial financial reports.

Infrastructure Investment

Is the city keeping up with the wear and tear on its roads, pipes, and buildings?

Capital Spending vs Amortization · 5-Year Average
--
Below 100% means infrastructure degrades faster than it's replaced. We use a 5-year average because capital spending is lumpy.

Reserves

The rainy-day fund. How long could the city operate without new revenue?

Months of Revenue in Reserve
--
Statutory reserves divided by monthly revenue. More months = more cushion.
Reserves Per Resident
--
Total statutory reserves divided by population.

Tax Burden

What are residents paying, and how dependent is the city on property taxes?

Residential Mill Rate
--
Tax per $1,000 of assessed home value. Lower is lighter on homeowners.
Tax Dependence
--
Share of revenue from property taxes. Higher = more reliant on homeowners.

Debt Burden

How much has the city borrowed, and how does that compare to what it earns?

Debt as % of Revenue
--
Long-term debt divided by annual revenue. Lower is healthier.
Debt Per Resident
--
Your share of the city's long-term borrowing.

Where the Money Goes

How Parksville allocates its budget compared to peers.

Protective Services Per Resident
--
RCMP + fire. Provincial data combines these; individual splits are in each city's financial plan.
Parks & Recreation Per Resident
--
Pools, arenas, parks, trails, community programs.
Administration Per Resident
--
Council, finance, HR, IT - the overhead of running a city.
Total Spending Per Resident
--
All expenses including amortization. Cities with water/sewer utilities will be higher.
