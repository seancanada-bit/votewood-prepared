How does Parksville's fiscal health compare to similar BC communities? All data comes from official BC Government financial reports - the same numbers auditors use.

🚧 Infrastructure Investment

Capital Spending vs Amortization
Amortization measures how fast infrastructure wears out. Capital spending is how much the city invests in replacing it. Below 100% means infrastructure is degrading faster than it's being replaced - an infrastructure deficit.

💰 Reserves - The Rainy-Day Fund

Reserves as Months of Revenue
How many months could the city operate on reserves alone? More months means more cushion for emergencies and capital projects without borrowing.
Reserves Per Capita
Statutory reserves (water, sewer, other) divided by population. The dollars set aside per resident.

🏠 Tax Burden

Residential Tax Rate (per $1,000 assessed)
The municipal mill rate applied to residential property. Higher means more tax per dollar of home value.
Tax Dependence
What share of total revenue comes from property taxes? Higher means the city relies more on homeowners and less on fees, grants, or other revenue.

📈 Debt Burden

Long-Term Debt as % of Revenue
How much of the city's annual revenue would it take to pay off all long-term debt? Lower is healthier.
Long-Term Debt Per Capita
Total long-term borrowing divided by population. Your share of the city's debt.

💲 Where the Money Goes

Protective Services Per Capita
RCMP contracts and fire departments combined. The provincial data does not separate police from fire - that breakdown is only in each city's individual financial plan.
Parks & Recreation Per Capita
Pools, arenas, parks, trails, and community programs. What you get back in quality of life.
General Government Per Capita
Administration, council, finance, HR, IT - the overhead of running a city.
Total Expenses Per Capita
Everything the city spends, including amortization. Context matters - cities with water/sewer utilities will be higher.
About This Data
All figures come from BC Government Local Government Statistics - the official financial reports every municipality is required to file. Schedules 302 (Liabilities), 304 (Financial Position), 401 (Revenue), 402 (Expenses), 404 (Changes in Net Financial Assets), and 707 (Tax Rates). Population from BC Stats estimates.
Communities were selected from across BC to represent a range of similar-sized cities and towns (population 3,000 to 39,000). Per-capita figures use BC Stats population estimates. Fiscal ratios use the most recent available annual financial data.

Source: BC Government Local Government Statistics · Open data
