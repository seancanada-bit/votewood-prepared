Process
How It Works

The City of Parksville runs two grant-in-aid intakes per year:

  • Spring intake — deadline in late February
  • Fall intake — deadline in late August

Applications are reviewed by council. Grants go to non-profit organizations serving the Parksville community. Application forms are available on parksville.ca.

The program is small in dollar terms but meaningful to the organizations that rely on it. Transparency about the process and outcomes matters.

Context
Historical Context

The grant-in-aid program has been modest in recent years. The spring 2025 allocation of $2,500 across five organizations reflects current budget realities.

In contrast, the spring 2020 intake was frozen entirely — $55K in applications were paused due to COVID budget constraints. Council created a separate $280K COVID Relief Grant-in-Aid program for non-profits to address the emergency.

The program reflects council's priorities about which community organizations receive public support and at what level. The allocations tell a story about what the city values.

An important distinction: the city receives major grants — for example, $2.97M in 2023 from federal and provincial programs for infrastructure projects.

The grant-in-aid program is what the city gives to local non-profits. Much smaller sums, but important for community organizations that depend on every dollar of support.

Both matter. One funds infrastructure, the other funds community.

Apply
How to Apply

Organizations must meet the following criteria:

  • Non-profit status — registered non-profit organization
  • Serve Parksville residents — programs or services benefiting the local community
  • Submit during intake periods — spring (late February) or fall (late August)

Contact the City of Parksville for application forms. Council reviews all applications and allocates based on available budget.
